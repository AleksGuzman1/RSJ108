Alessandro Guzman's How To Get Started In Sports Journalism

An Instruction Challenge for JOUR108

The route to becoming the best and most interesting sports journalist ...

Assembly time: 1-2 Hours
Awesome rating: 100% Awesome

Picture this: A career where you travel the country watching your favorite sports teams live doccumenting your opinions and what you see for sports fans all over America to deliberate over. Following these steps will get you started in becoming a worthy sports journalist like Stephen A. Smith and Shannon Sharpe!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Laptop
  • Camera
  • Editing Software
  • Internet Access
  • Sports Knowledge
  • Creativity/Open-mindedness
  • Communication Skills (Marketing oppurtunities)

  1. Honing Your Writing Skills.

    Start a blog, read, join sports subreddits, do all that you can so you can finely tune up your writing skills. A smoothly written paper makes it easier for the reader to be attracted the entire time.

  2. Obtain Training In Journalism

    Pursuing a degree in journalism is the best way to obtain basic knowledge with everything journalism. A degree isn't required to become a sports journalist, but a degree gives you's an extra boost when looking for work. All in all, it's important you familiarize with the field and practice as much as you can.

    .

  3. Specialize In Sports Journalism

    In order to write about sports, you need to learn about sports. Watch and attend as many sports games as you can, memeorize coach and player names. Watch interviews, podcasts, follow social media accounts that relate to sports. It's crucial that you develop a strong knowledge for sports as you want your fanbase to be able to trust your opinions. Another important thing to realize is something is always happen in every sport whether or not the season is active. Just like normal news, it's important you're able to deliver important news to your readers as fast as possible. It's time to become a sports geek.

  4. Landing A Sports Journalist Job

    Journalism itself is a competative field no matter the emphasis. It's important you find a rhythm that fits you best- whether that be through freelance work or through internships as they both offer their own edges. After publishing several pieces it's important you build a portfolio- make sure your strongest and best pieces are included. Remember throughout your entire journey to network yourself and your work in everyway possible. Create relationships with everyone along the way, bosses, co-workers, and friends, all relationships can lead to job oppurtunities. CONSISTENCY IS KEY!

  5. BE YOU & LOVE WHAT YOU DO!

    Remember, the biggest sports journalist today are in the position that they are in because they act like themselves- they're not afarid to express how they feel and it's why they have strong fan bases. Always be free and enjoy what you're doing, never feel like you have to act or speak a certain way because of anything. No one appreciates a fake person and fake opinions.
